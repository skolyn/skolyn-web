1. Overview

This Quick Start Guide walks you through the fastest path to getting Skolyn operational in your environment. By the end of this guide, you will have connected your PACS, processed your first imaging study through the AI pipeline, reviewed the AI-generated findings, and verified integration with your reporting system. This guide assumes you have a Skolyn cloud-hosted subscription or have completed the on-premises installation (see SKL-GS-003: Installation Guide). The entire process takes approximately 25-30 minutes for an experienced healthcare IT professional.

Prerequisites: Active Skolyn account, PACS with DICOMweb or C-STORE capability, network connectivity to Skolyn endpoints (port 443 HTTPS, port 11112 DICOM TLS), at least one test DICOM study available for processing.

2. Step-by-Step Setup

1

Access the Management Console

Navigate to your Skolyn instance URL (e.g., https://your-org.skolyn.cloud) and sign in with your administrator credentials. If your organization uses SSO, you will be redirected to your identity provider (Microsoft Entra ID, Okta, etc.) for authentication. Upon first login, you will be prompted to set up MFA using a TOTP authenticator app or hardware security key.

After authentication, you will land on the Dashboard showing system status, pending configuration tasks, and getting-started checklist widgets. The checklist provides real-time progress tracking through the initial setup process.

2

Configure PACS Connection

Navigate to Settings > Integrations > DICOM Sources and click Add Source. Configure your PACS connection with the following parameters:

ParameterDescriptionExample
Source NameDisplay name for this connection"Main PACS - GE Centricity"

Click Test Connection to verify C-ECHO (or WADO-RS ping for DICOMweb). A green checkmark confirms successful connectivity. If the test fails, verify network routes, firewall rules, and AE Title registration on your PACS.

3

Configure Result Destination

Navigate to Settings > Integrations > Result Destinations to configure where Skolyn delivers AI findings. Supported destination types include:

  • DICOM SR (Structured Report): Push findings as DICOM Structured Reports back to your PACS. The SR is associated with the original study and appears as an additional series in your viewer.
  • HL7v2 ORU: Send results as HL7 ORU^R01 messages to your RIS or EHR. Fields map to standard OBR/OBX segments with configurable observation identifiers.
  • FHIR DiagnosticReport: POST structured findings to your FHIR R4 server as DiagnosticReport resources with linked Observation resources for each finding.
  • PDF Report: Generate formatted PDF reports delivered via secure email or available for download from the Management Console.

For the quickest setup, we recommend configuring a DICOM SR destination to your existing PACS. This requires no changes to your reporting workflow — the AI findings simply appear alongside the study in your viewer.

4

Select Active Modules

Navigate to Settings > Modules to activate the AI analysis modules relevant to your clinical needs. Each module can be enabled independently:

  • Rhenium OS (MRI): Enable specific subtypes (Brain, Cardiac, Breast, MSK, etc.) based on your exam volume and clinical priorities.
  • Seaborgium OS (CT): Enable CT subtypes with optional STAT triage for critical findings.
  • Scandium OS (Ultrasound): Enable real-time streaming analysis for ultrasound exams.
  • Terbium OS (X-Ray): Enable projection radiography analysis with critical finding alerts.

Each subtype can be configured with a processing priority (Normal, High, STAT) and threshold settings for alerting sensitivity. We recommend starting with one modality, validating the workflow, and then expanding to additional modules.

5

Submit Your First Study

With PACS connectivity configured and modules activated, submit a test study for AI analysis. There are three methods:

  • Automatic Routing: Configure your PACS to auto-route new studies to Skolyn's AE Title (SKOLYN_AI). Studies matching the enabled module criteria are processed automatically.
  • Manual Push: From your PACS, C-STORE an individual study to the Skolyn AE Title.
  • Console Upload: In the Management Console, navigate to Studies > Upload and drag-and-drop DICOM files for immediate processing.

Once submitted, the study appears in the Processing Queue with a real-time status indicator. Processing typically completes within 15-30 seconds for a standard study. Upon completion, the status changes to "Complete" and results are delivered to your configured destination.

6

Review AI Findings

Open the processed study in your PACS viewer. The Skolyn AI findings appear as a new series (DICOM SR) within the study. The structured report includes:

  • Summary Section: Overall assessment with key findings highlighted and prioritized by clinical significance.
  • Detailed Findings: Each finding includes location, description, measurements, confidence score, and differential diagnosis.
  • Attention Maps: Grad-CAM++ heatmap overlays showing exactly which image regions influenced the AI's assessment.
  • Comparison Data: If prior studies are available, temporal comparison results showing change analysis.
Success! You have completed the Quick Start setup. Skolyn is now actively analyzing incoming studies, delivering AI-powered insights directly into your radiology workflow.
