Trauma CT Guide

Seaborgium OS Module - Version 2.0 - Last updated: February 2026

1. Overview

The Trauma CT analysis module in Seaborgium OS is engineered for high-speed, comprehensive detection of acute traumatic injuries. Designed for the "Golden Hour" of trauma care, it processes whole-body trauma CT scans (Pan-Scan) in under 3 minutes, identifying life-threatening conditions such as hemorrhage, organ injury, fractures, and vascular dissection.

The system prioritizes findings based on clinical urgency, instantly alerting trauma teams to critical pathologies via PACS, mobile app, and secure messaging.

2. Head & Neck Trauma

2.1 Intracranial Hemorrhage

Automated detection and volumetric quantification of all hemorrhage subtypes:

  • Epidural Hematoma: Convex/lens-shaped, does not cross sutures
  • Subdural Hematoma: Concave/crescentic, crosses sutures
  • Subarachnoid Hemorrhage: High density within sulci/cisterns
  • Intraparenchymal Hemorrhage: Focal hyperdensity with/without edema
  • Mass Effect: Midline shift measurement (>5mm alerts neurosurgery)

2.2 Cervical Spine Injury

Comprehensive fracture detection and alignment analysis:

  • Fractures: Vertebral body, posterior elements, odontoid process (Type I/II/III)
  • Alignment: Atlanto-occipital dislocation (CCI > 2.5mm), subluxation, spondylolisthesis
  • Ligament: Prevertebral soft tissue swelling measurement

3. Thoracic Trauma

3.1 Vascular Injury

Detection of major vascular trauma:

  • Aortic Transection: Mediastinal hematoma, contour irregularity, intimal flap
  • Active Bleeding: Contrast extravasation (blush) in arterial/venous phase

3.2 Pulmonary Injury

Quantification of lung and pleural space injuries:

  • Pneumothorax: Detection (deep sulcus sign), depth measurement, occult vs. overt
  • Hemothorax: Pleural fluid density analysis (>30 HU), volume estimation
  • Contusion: Classification of lung parenchymal injury pattern
  • Rib Fractures: Automated detection and mapping (flail chest identification)

4. Abdominal & Pelvic Trauma

4.1 Solid Organ Injury

AAST (American Association for the Surgery of Trauma) grading for liver, spleen, and kidney injuries:

OrganGrading CriteriaExample
SpleenLaceration depth, subcapsular hematoma %Grade III: Laceration >3cm depth
LiverLaceration depth, hematoma %, vascular injuryGrade IV: Disrupting >50% of lobe
KidneyLaceration depth, collecting system involv.Grade IV: Urinary extravasation

4.2 Pelvic Fractures

Young-Burgess classification of pelvic ring fractures:

  • Lateral Compression: LC-1, LC-2, LC-3
  • Anteroposterior Compression: APC-1, APC-2, APC-3 ("Open Book")
  • Vertical Shear: Vertical displacement of hemipelvis
  • Acetabular Fractures: Judet-Letournel classification support

4.3 Hemorrhage Detection

Automated detection of:

  • Active Extravasation: High-density contrast pooling
  • Hemoperitoneum: Free fluid density analysis (30-45 HU) in dependent regions (Morison's pouch)

5. Spine & Musculoskeletal

5.1 Thoracolumbar Spine

Automated detection of burst fractures, chance fractures, and compression deformities. Evaluation of stability using TLICS (Thoracolumbar Injury Classification and Severity) scoring parameters (morphology, neurological status, ligamentous complex).

5.2 Extremity Fractures

Detection of long bone fractures (femur, tibia, humerus) and major joint dislocations (hip, knee, shoulder). Identification of vascular compromise associated with fractures (e.g., popliteal artery in knee dislocation).
