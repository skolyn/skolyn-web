1. Introduction

The Radiology Information System (RIS) is the operational backbone of radiology departments, managing the lifecycle of imaging orders from scheduling through acquisition, interpretation, and reporting. Skolyn's RIS integration embeds AI analysis seamlessly into this workflow, ensuring that AI findings are available to radiologists at the point of interpretation without adding extra steps, logins, or context switches. This document covers order receipt workflows, worklist management, AI-augmented reporting, result status synchronization, and vendor-specific configuration for leading RIS platforms including Cerner RadNet, Epic Radiant, AGFA HealthCare, and Sectra RIS.

2. Order-Driven Processing

2.1 Order Receipt via HL7v2 ORM

Skolyn receives radiology orders from the RIS via HL7v2 ORM^O01 messages. The order message provides critical context that guides AI analysis: procedure code (mapping to the appropriate AI module and subtype), clinical indication (used for context-aware AI analysis and report generation), ordering provider (for result notification routing), and patient demographics (for demographic-adjusted analysis thresholds). Skolyn correlates the HL7 order with the incoming DICOM study using the Accession Number (OBR-18 field matched against DICOM tag 0008,0050) to ensure accurate study-to-order linking.

3. Worklist Integration

3.1 AI-Augmented Worklist

Skolyn enriches the radiologist's reading worklist with AI processing status and preliminary findings. When a study has been analyzed by Skolyn prior to the radiologist opening it, the worklist entry displays an AI status indicator (complete/pending/not-applicable), finding count, critical finding flag, and a confidence score summary. This allows radiologists to prioritize their reading queue based on AI triage — studies with critical AI findings can be promoted to the top of the worklist automatically.

3.2 DICOM Modality Worklist (MWL)

For sites where the RIS manages the DICOM Modality Worklist, Skolyn participates as a Worklist Management SCP, providing AI-specific worklist entries for quality assurance workflows. QA technologists and AI validation radiologists can query Skolyn's MWL to retrieve studies that require human review of AI findings, filtered by module, finding type, confidence level, or review status.

4. AI-Augmented Reporting

4.1 Prefilled Report Templates

Skolyn generates prefilled radiology report templates based on AI findings, formatted according to the institution's reporting style guide. The templates include standardized finding descriptions, measurements, relevant prior comparison data, and differential diagnosis suggestions. Radiologists can accept, modify, or discard the AI-generated content within their existing dictation or structured reporting workflow. Template generation supports RadLex Playbook procedure descriptions and ACR Reporting Templates for structured reporting compliance.

4.2 Report Addendum

When Skolyn completes analysis after the radiologist has already begun or finalized their report, AI findings are delivered as a structured addendum suggestion via HL7v2 ORU message. The addendum includes a clear "AI-Generated" label, a disclaimer regarding the supplementary nature of AI findings, and a reference to the original report. The radiologist reviews and decides whether to incorporate the addendum into the final report.

6. Result Status Synchronization

Skolyn maintains bidirectional status synchronization with the RIS throughout the study lifecycle:

  • Order Placed: Skolyn receives ORM and prepares for incoming study (pre-fetches prior studies if configured).
  • Study Acquired: DICOM study arrives; Skolyn begins AI analysis. Status: "AI Processing."
  • AI Complete: Analysis finished; findings delivered. Status: "AI Results Available."
  • Radiologist Review: Radiologist opens the study in PACS viewer. AI findings displayed alongside images.
  • Report Finalized: Skolyn receives notification (ORU with status "F") and archives the case for quality metrics.
  • Addendum Added: If late-arriving AI findings supplement a finalized report, the addendum workflow is triggered.

7. Quality Metrics Dashboard

Skolyn tracks AI-to-radiologist concordance metrics per RIS integration: agreement rate (AI finding confirmed by radiologist), miss rate (AI finding not present in final report), false positive rate, and turnaround time impact. These metrics are available via the Management Console (Analytics > RIS Integration) and exportable for departmental quality improvement programs.
