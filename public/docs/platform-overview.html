1. Introduction

Skolyn is an enterprise-grade medical imaging artificial intelligence platform designed to augment radiologist workflows through explainable, clinically validated diagnostic analysis. The platform processes imaging studies across four modalities — Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Computed Tomography (CT), Ultrasound (US), and X-Ray — delivering AI-generated findings, measurements, and structured reports directly into existing clinical workflows via seamless PACS, RIS, and EHR integration.

Unlike opaque black-box AI systems, Skolyn is built on the principle of Explainable AI (XAI), providing clinicians with visual attention maps (Grad-CAM++, SHAP), confidence scores, differential diagnoses, and evidence-based reasoning for every finding. This transparency builds clinical trust and satisfies regulatory requirements for AI-assisted diagnostics under FDA, CE MDR, and PMDA frameworks.

Mission: To reduce diagnostic error, accelerate turnaround time, and democratize access to expert-level radiology by embedding explainable AI directly into the clinical imaging workflow.

2. Platform Architecture

2.1 High-Level Architecture

Skolyn follows a cloud-native, microservices-based architecture deployed on Kubernetes. The system is organized into five functional layers:

  • Ingestion Layer: Receives DICOM studies from PACS via C-STORE SCP, DICOMweb STOW-RS, or file-based import. Studies are validated, de-duplicated, and queued for processing. The ingestion engine supports concurrent processing of over 10,000 studies per hour with automatic load balancing across inference nodes.
  • Preprocessing Layer: Normalizes pixel data (rescale, window/level), applies modality-specific preprocessing (e.g., EPI distortion correction for fMRI, beam hardening correction for CT), and segments anatomical regions using atlas-based and deep learning methods. Preprocessing results are cached to avoid redundant computation for follow-up analyses.
  • Inference Layer: The core AI engine running modality-specific models. Each module (Rhenium, Seaborgium, Scandium, Terbium) is a containerized ensemble of specialized sub-models optimized for specific body regions and clinical tasks. Inference runs on GPU-accelerated nodes (NVIDIA A100/H100) with automatic scaling based on queue depth.
  • Integration Layer: Delivers results back to the clinical environment via DICOM SR (Structured Report), HL7v2 ORU messages, FHIR DiagnosticReport resources, and PDF reports. Integration adapters handle facility-specific formatting, terminology mapping (SNOMED CT, RadLex, ICD-10), and priority routing for critical findings.
  • Presentation Layer: An optional web-based viewer and dashboard providing real-time status monitoring, AI finding review, model performance analytics, and administrative functions.

3. Imaging Modules

3.1 Rhenium OS (MRI)

The Rhenium module processes 14 MRI subtypes including Brain, Cardiac, Breast, MSK, Spine, Prostate, Whole-Body, Vascular MRA, Fetal, Liver, Head & Neck, Pelvic, Abdominal, and Spectroscopy MRI. Key capabilities include cross-attention multi-sequence fusion (combining T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI, and contrast-enhanced sequences), volumetric 3D analysis with temporal comparison to prior studies, and structured reporting with standardized scoring systems (PI-RADS, BI-RADS, mRECIST). The module achieves sub-millimeter spatial precision for lesion measurement and provides attention-weighted heatmaps for every finding.

3.2 Seaborgium OS (CT)

The Seaborgium module handles 13 CT subtypes spanning Brain, Chest, Abdominal, Vascular CTA, Cardiac CTA, Head & Neck, Spine, MSK, Dental CBCT, Virtual Colonoscopy, Trauma Whole-Body, Pediatric, and Low-Dose Screening CT. The module features a STAT triage pipeline that prioritizes critical findings (large vessel occlusion, pneumothorax, aortic dissection) for immediate radiologist attention with a median alert latency under 90 seconds from study receipt. 3D volumetric processing enables accurate nodule volumetry, calcium scoring (Agatston), and longitudinal growth tracking with Lung-RADS classification.

3.3 Scandium OS (Ultrasound)

The Scandium module supports 12 ultrasound subtypes including Echocardiography, Obstetric, Thyroid, Breast, Abdominal, Vascular, Neonatal Cranial, CEUS, Gynecological, Scrotal, Renal, Lung, Pediatric, and Interventional US. Designed for real-time streaming analysis, the module processes video-rate ultrasound frames (30+ fps) with speckle-aware preprocessing that enhances tissue boundaries without amplifying noise. Key scoring systems include TI-RADS for thyroid, BI-RADS for breast, and Simpson's biplane for cardiac ejection fraction.

3.4 Terbium OS (X-Ray)

The Terbium module covers 10 X-Ray subtypes: Chest, Bone/MSK, Spine, Joint, Abdominal, Dental, DXA Bone Density, Mammography, Pediatric, and Bone Age Assessment. The multi-scale encoder architecture processes both low-resolution overview features and high-resolution local details, enabling simultaneous detection of subtle findings (hairline fractures, early nodules) and global patterns (cardiomegaly, scoliosis). The STAT alert system provides critical finding notifications for pneumothorax, pneumoperitoneum, and displaced fractures within 60 seconds.

4. Key Differentiators

  • Explainability First: Every AI finding includes visual attention maps, confidence intervals, alternative diagnoses, and supporting evidence citations from peer-reviewed literature.
  • Modality Coverage: Four dedicated modules covering 49 clinical subtypes across MRI, CT, US, and X-Ray — the broadest coverage of any single medical imaging AI platform.
  • Workflow Integration: Zero-friction deployment into existing PACS/RIS/EHR infrastructure via standard protocols (DICOM, HL7, FHIR). No parallel workflows required.
  • Clinical Validation: Prospective multi-institutional clinical trials with published results demonstrating non-inferior performance to board-certified radiologists across key diagnostic tasks.
  • Regulatory Compliance: FDA 510(k) cleared, CE MDR Class IIa certified, HIPAA compliant, GDPR compliant, SOC 2 Type II certified, ISO 27001 and ISO 13485 certified.

6. Deployment Options

Skolyn supports three deployment models to accommodate varying infrastructure requirements and data sovereignty concerns:

  • Cloud-Hosted (SaaS): Fully managed by Skolyn on dedicated cloud infrastructure. Available regions: US-East, US-West, EU-West (Frankfurt), EU-West (Paris), APAC (Tokyo, Sydney). Data residency guarantees with region-locked processing.
  • On-Premises: Deployed within the healthcare facility's own data center. Skolyn provides Kubernetes-based deployment packages, GPU hardware specifications, and remote management tooling. Full air-gap support available for classified environments.
  • Hybrid: Core inference runs on-premises for data sovereignty; analytics, model updates, and management plane hosted in the cloud. Secure VPN connectivity with end-to-end encryption.
