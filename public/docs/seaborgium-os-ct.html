1. Module Overview

Seaborgium OS is Skolyn's high-performance CT analysis module, optimized for the massive volumetric datasets that characterize modern multi-detector CT imaging. Named after Seaborgium (Sg, atomic number 106), a synthetic superheavy element, the module reflects the engineered precision required to analyze high-resolution isotropic CT datasets with 1,000+ slices per study. Seaborgium OS supports 13 anatomical subtypes spanning chest, abdomen, pelvis, head, neck, cardiac, and musculoskeletal CT applications. The module's hallmark features include STAT triage for emergency CT exams, 3D volumetric segmentation, Lung-RADS-compliant pulmonary nodule management, fully automated coronary calcium scoring, and sub-15-second processing times for chest CT studies.

3. Architecture

3.1 3D Volumetric Processing

Seaborgium OS processes CT data in native 3D volumes rather than slice-by-slice, leveraging 3D convolutional neural networks (3D-ResNet-152 backbone with Feature Pyramid Network) that capture axial, coronal, and sagittal relationships simultaneously. For large studies exceeding GPU memory, the engine uses a sliding window approach with overlapping 3D patches (patch size 128x128x128, overlap 32 voxels) and seamless stitching via Gaussian-weighted merging at patch boundaries. This 3D approach is critical for accurate detection of findings that span multiple slices — pulmonary emboli coursing through vessels, compression fractures with subtle cortical disruption, and diffuse hepatic steatosis patterns.

3.2 STAT Triage Engine

The STAT triage engine is a lightweight, fast-executing classification network that runs before the full analysis pipeline. Within 3 seconds of study receipt, the triage engine assigns a priority score (1-5) based on the probability of life-threatening findings (intracranial hemorrhage, pulmonary embolism, aortic dissection, pneumothorax, large vessel occlusion). Studies scoring priority 1 or 2 trigger immediate STAT alerts to the on-call radiologist and referring clinician, and are promoted to the front of the processing queue for full analysis. The triage engine achieves 98.5% sensitivity for critical findings with a 5% false positive rate, validated on 50,000 emergency CT studies.

5. Pulmonary Nodule Management

Seaborgium OS implements Lung-RADS v2022 classification with automated volumetric tracking across serial studies. Each detected nodule is characterized by location (lobe, segment), morphology (solid, part-solid, ground-glass), attenuation statistics (mean, max HU), volumetric measurement (with sub-voxel interpolation), and growth rate calculation when prior studies are available. Volume doubling time is computed using exponential growth modeling, and Lung-RADS category is assigned based on nodule type, size, and growth pattern. Structured reporting includes recommended follow-up interval per Lung-RADS guidelines.
