1. Executive Summary

For European Economic Area (EEA) customers and any organization processing personal data of EU/EEA residents, Skolyn ensures full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679). As a medical imaging AI platform processing special category data (health data under Art. 9), Skolyn implements robust technical and organizational measures to protect data subject rights, enforce data minimization, and ensure lawful processing. This document details Skolyn's role as a Data Processor, its technical capabilities for supporting Data Controllers, and the complete framework for managing data subject rights, international transfers, Data Protection Impact Assessments, and breach notification procedures.

2.2 Legal Bases for Processing (Art. 6 & Art. 9)

Processing of health data by Skolyn relies on the following legal bases, as determined by the Data Controller:

  • Art. 6(1)(b) — Contract Performance: Processing necessary for the provision of diagnostic AI services under the service agreement.
  • Art. 9(2)(h) — Healthcare Provision: Processing necessary for the provision of healthcare, medical diagnosis, or management of health systems under the responsibility of a healthcare professional subject to medical secrecy.
  • Art. 6(1)(f) — Legitimate Interest: Processing for security monitoring, fraud prevention, and system improvement (anonymized analytics only).
  • Art. 9(2)(j) — Scientific Research: Where applicable, processing for AI model improvement using de-identified data with appropriate safeguards per Art. 89.

3. Data Subject Rights

Skolyn provides automated technical tools to help Data Controllers fulfill requests from Data Subjects (patients) within the mandated timeframes:

3.1 Right of Access (Art. 15)

The Patient Data Export feature generates a comprehensive ZIP archive containing: all DICOM images associated with the Patient ID, AI-generated diagnostic reports, structured findings in FHIR JSON format, complete audit trail showing who accessed the records, and a human-readable summary document. Controllers can initiate exports via the Admin Console or REST API. Export processing completes within 24 hours for standard requests.

3.2 Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

When patient demographics are incorrect, Skolyn supports rectification through HL7 ADT messages (ADT^A08 — Update Patient Information) or manual correction via the Management Console. Changes propagate to all associated study records, reports, and audit logs within 60 seconds. A rectification log entry is created documenting the before/after state for compliance audit purposes.

3.3 Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

The Hard Delete function permanently removes all data for a specified patient from: active database records, search indices (OpenSearch), object storage (DICOM files, reports), cached inference results, and backup archives (after retention period expiration). A cryptographic certificate of destruction is generated, documenting the timestamp, scope, operator, and verification hash. The process is irreversible and requires dual authorization (Controller Administrator + Skolyn DPO confirmation).

3.4 Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18)

Administrators can mark specific studies or patient records as Restricted. Restricted data is excluded from AI processing queues, search results, and general user access while preserved in encrypted cold storage for legal or regulatory purposes. Restriction status is visible in the audit trail and can be lifted only by the original Controller Administrator.

3.5 Right to Data Portability (Art. 20)

Skolyn supports data export in multiple interoperable formats: DICOM (native imaging format), HL7 FHIR R4 JSON (structured reports), PDF (human-readable reports), and CSV (tabular data exports). All exports are delivered via encrypted channels and can be streamed directly to a receiving system via DICOMweb STOW-RS or FHIR API endpoints.

3.6 Right to Object (Art. 21)

Data Subjects may object to automated decision-making processes. Skolyn provides a configuration option to flag studies requiring mandatory human review before any AI-generated findings are included in the final diagnostic report, ensuring meaningful human oversight per Art. 22.

4. Privacy by Design & Default (Art. 25)

  • Data Minimization: Skolyn retrieves only the minimum data necessary for the requested analysis. Prior studies are fetched only when clinically relevant (matching body region and modality). Patient demographics beyond the minimum required for study matching are not loaded into memory.
  • Pseudonymization: Internal processing pipelines use hashed identifiers (SHA-256 of Patient ID + salt). The mapping table between pseudonyms and real identifiers is stored separately with restricted access.
  • Storage Limitation: Configurable automated data lifecycle policies allow Controllers to define rules such as "Delete studies older than 10 years," "Anonymize after 5 years," or "Archive to cold storage after 2 years." These policies are enforced automatically by the platform's data lifecycle engine.
  • Purpose Limitation: PHI is processed exclusively for the documented purpose (clinical AI analysis). Secondary use for model training requires explicit de-identification and separate legal basis documentation.

5.2 Transfer Safeguards

For deployments requiring data transfer outside the EEA, Skolyn employs the following mechanisms:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): The DPA incorporates the latest EU Commission-approved SCCs (Module 2: Controller-to-Processor) as the primary transfer mechanism.
  • Supplementary Measures: Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256), pseudonymization, and access controls that prevent foreign government access to unencrypted PHI.
  • Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA): Skolyn provides documentation to support Controllers in conducting TIAs per EDPB Recommendations 01/2020.

6. Data Protection Impact Assessment (Art. 35)

Skolyn provides comprehensive DPIA documentation covering:

  • Systematic Description: Automated diagnostic imaging analysis using deep learning on medical images containing special category health data.
  • Necessity & Proportionality: AI analysis directly supports clinical decision-making, improving diagnostic accuracy and reducing turnaround time. Only minimum necessary data is processed.
  • Risk Assessment: Identified risks include algorithmic bias (mitigated by diverse training data and fairness audits), data breach (mitigated by defense-in-depth security), and false positive/negative results (mitigated by human-in-the-loop validation).
  • Safeguards: Technical measures (encryption, access control, audit logging), organizational measures (training, DPO oversight, incident response), and clinical safeguards (human validation, explainability features).

7. Breach Notification (Art. 33 & 34)

Skolyn commits to notifying the Data Controller without undue delay and within 24 hours of confirming a personal data breach. The notification report includes:

  • Nature of the breach and categories of data affected
  • Approximate number of data subjects and records concerned
  • Name and contact details of Skolyn's Data Protection Officer
  • Likely consequences of the breach
  • Measures taken and proposed to address and mitigate the breach
  • Forensic timeline and root cause analysis (provided within 72 hours)

8. Data Protection Officer

Skolyn has appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) in accordance with Art. 37. The DPO is accessible to data subjects, supervisory authorities, and Data Controllers for all matters relating to the processing of personal data and GDPR compliance. Contact: dpo@skolyn.com
