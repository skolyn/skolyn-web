Cardiac CT Analysis

Coronary CT Angiography, Calcium Scoring, and FFR-CT Estimation
Version: 3.0
Last Updated: February 2026
Classification: Confidential
Owner: CT Engineering

1. Overview

The Cardiac CT analysis subtype provides comprehensive assessment of coronary CT angiography (CCTA), coronary artery calcium (CAC) scoring, and cardiac structural evaluation. Cardiac CT has emerged as a frontline non-invasive test for chest pain evaluation following the landmark PROMISE and SCOT-HEART trials, and is now recommended as a Class I indication by the American College of Cardiology and American Heart Association guidelines for stable chest pain. Skolyn's cardiac CT AI automates the historically time-consuming process of coronary artery analysis — traditional manual processing requires 30-60 minutes per study — delivering comprehensive results in under 30 seconds with automated coronary artery segmentation, stenosis grading, plaque characterization, and AI-driven FFR-CT (fractional flow reserve derived from CT) estimation for functional significance assessment.

2. Coronary Artery Calcium Scoring

The CAC scoring module processes non-contrast ECG-gated cardiac CT datasets to compute the Agatston score, the gold standard for coronary artery calcification quantification. The algorithm automatically identifies and segments calcified deposits (threshold >130 HU across at least 3 contiguous pixels) within the coronary arteries, assigns them to the four standard coronary vessels (left main, LAD, LCx, RCA), and computes per-vessel and total Agatston scores. Results include risk stratification by percentile ranking adjusted for age, sex, and ethnicity (using the MESA — Multi-Ethnic Study of Atherosclerosis — normative database). CAC categories are reported as zero (very low risk), 1-100 (mild), 101-400 (moderate), and >400 (severe), with associated 10-year ASCVD event rates for clinical decision support.

3. Coronary CTA Analysis

3.1 Automated Segmentation

The coronary artery segmentation engine extracts the coronary arterial tree from contrast-enhanced cardiac CT using a graph-cut-based vessel tracking algorithm initialized by deep learning-based ostial detection. The engine segments all 18 AHA/ACC coronary segments (per the modified AHA 15-segment model plus proximal, mid, and distal RCA subsegments) and generates straightened multiplanar reformations (curved MPR), cross-sectional views, and 3D volume renderings for each vessel. Segmentation handles challenging anatomy including anomalous coronary origins, myocardial bridging, and post-CABG anatomy with identification and tracing of saphenous vein and internal mammary artery grafts.

3.2 Stenosis Grading

Each coronary segment is evaluated for luminal stenosis with grading following the CAD-RADS (Coronary Artery Disease — Reporting and Data System) classification. CAD-RADS 0 — no stenosis (0%). CAD-RADS 1 — minimal (1-24%). CAD-RADS 2 — mild (25-49%). CAD-RADS 3 — moderate (50-69%). CAD-RADS 4A — severe (70-99%). CAD-RADS 4B — left main ≥50% or three-vessel ≥70%. CAD-RADS 5 — total occlusion (100%). Modifiers include V (vulnerability features), G (graft), S (stent), and N (non-diagnostic segment). Stenosis measurement uses minimal lumen area (MLA) and minimal lumen diameter (MLD) compared to the reference vessel diameter, computed from cross-sectional analysis at the point of maximum narrowing.

4. Plaque Characterization

Beyond stenosis grading, the module performs detailed plaque analysis including attenuation-based composition classification (calcified >350 HU, fibrous 75-350 HU, lipid-rich/necrotic <30 HU), plaque volume quantification (total, calcified, non-calcified, low-attenuation), and identification of high-risk plaque features. High-risk features include the "napkin-ring sign" (rim of high attenuation surrounding a low-attenuation core), positive remodeling (remodeling index >1.1), low attenuation plaque (<30 HU), and spotty calcification. The pericoronary fat attenuation index (FAI) is computed for each major vessel as a biomarker of vascular inflammation, with values > -70 HU indicating elevated inflammation risk per the CRISP-CT trial data.

5. FFR-CT Estimation

The AI-driven FFR-CT module provides non-invasive estimation of fractional flow reserve for each coronary territory. Unlike computational fluid dynamics (CFD)-based approaches that require significant processing time, Skolyn's FFR-CT estimation uses a deep learning model trained on paired CCTA/invasive FFR datasets (10,000+ vessels) to predict vessel-specific FFR values in under 10 seconds. An FFR-CT value ≤0.80 indicates hemodynamically significant stenosis warranting consideration of revascularization, consistent with the invasive FFR threshold established by the FAME and FAME-2 trials. This capability allows comprehensive anatomical and functional assessment from a single CCTA acquisition.
